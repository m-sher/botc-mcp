# Death and Ghost Players

## Core principle

**Death is not the end.** Dead players remain full social participants and share
their team’s victory or defeat.

## How players die

| Cause | Typical timing | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Demon attack | Night | Announced at dawn; method not revealed |
| Execution | Day | At most one per day |
| Ability kills | Day or night | Slayer, Virgin bounce, curses, etc. |
| Storyteller-triggered | Varies | e.g. Gossip true statement (other scripts) |

## Effects of death

| Keep | Lose |
| --- | --- |
| Ability to talk any time | Character ability (usually immediately) |
| Team win/loss | Right to **nominate** |
| One **ghost vote** for the rest of the game | Unlimited voting |
| Closing eyes at night | Waking for night abilities (usually) |

### Exceptions

Some abilities explicitly work **when you die** or **even if dead**
(e.g. Ravenkeeper learns a character if killed at night).
Those still fail if the player is drunk/poisoned at the critical moment
(unless text says otherwise).

## Dead players cannot die again

- Demon attacking a dead player does not re-kill them and is not announced as a death.
- Useful for Imp “sink kills” while bluffing Soldier/Monk (no public death).

## Ghost vote logistics

1. On death, player receives a single remaining vote (marked on Town Square).
2. They may cast it on **one** nomination ever (any future day).
3. After casting, remove the vote token — they cannot vote again.
4. They still cannot nominate.

## Public information on death

At dawn / on execution, the group learns **who** died, not:

- Who killed them
- Whether it was Demon vs ability
- Their character or alignment

## Life count for win conditions

- “Living players” for the **two-alive evil win** excludes Travellers (standard rule).
- Dead do not count as living for vote majority thresholds either
  (thresholds use living count only).
