# Ability Resolution

## Authority

1. Character ability text in [roles/](roles/)
2. Night order as wake/reminder sequence ([night-order.md](night-order.md))

## Immediate resolution

Abilities resolve **when their condition is met**.

Examples:

- Imp kills Fortune Teller before FT’s slot → FT does not act that night.
- Monk protects Empath before Imp attacks Empath → Empath lives.

## Information hygiene

- Only tell a player what **their** ability says they learn.
- The group does **not** learn: SW conversion, whether Slayer hit, Monk protection,
  who caused a night death, etc., unless an ability explicitly informs someone.

## Loss of ability

Lost **immediately** on death (unless text triggers on death / works while dead),
drunkenness, or poisoning.

Persistent effects from a dead or ability-less source end
(e.g. Poisoner dies → current poison ends).

## “Choose” vs Storyteller choice

| Wording | Who decides |
| --- | --- |
| Player **chooses** | That player |
| No chooser named | Storyteller (e.g. which Minion gets starpass, Mayor bounce target) |

## Illegal choices

Reject and force re-choose (Monk cannot pick self, etc.).

## Registration

When detecting type or alignment, apply Spy / Recluse / red herring registration first.
See [states.md](states.md).
