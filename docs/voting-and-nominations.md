# Nominations, Voting, and Execution

Applies during the **Day** after discussion. Storyteller controls when nominations open.

## Core constraints

| Rule | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Nominations per living player | **Once** per day |
| Times a player may be nominated | **Once** per day |
| Executions per day | **At most one** |
| Must there be an execution? | **No** — nominations can all fail or none may occur |
| Dead players nominate? | **No** |
| Dead players vote? | **Yes**, but only **one vote total for the rest of the game** (ghost vote) |

## Nomination procedure

1. Storyteller opens nominations; players return to seats.
2. A living player says: “I nominate **X**.”
3. Storyteller repeats the nomination for clarity.
4. Nominee may defend (no fixed time limit).
5. Vote proceeds immediately after defense.

### Virgin interaction (Trouble Brewing)

The **first** time the Virgin is nominated:

- If the nominator is a **Townsfolk**, the nominator is **immediately executed**
  (no vote). That is the day’s execution; day ends for further nominations.
- If the nominator is Outsider / Minion / Demon, nothing special — vote normally.
- After the first nomination of the Virgin (regardless of outcome), the ability is spent.

## Voting procedure

1. Storyteller faces the nominee and sweeps **clockwise**.
2. Each player with hand **up** when the Storyteller reaches them casts a vote.
3. Living players may vote on **as many** nominations as they wish that day
   (one hand per nomination).
4. Dead players may cast their **single remaining ghost vote** on one nomination
   sometime that day (or a future day) — once used, gone.
5. Storyteller counts votes aloud; nominee votes last in the sweep.

## Execution threshold

A nomination is a **contender for execution** only if:

1. Votes ≥ **half the number of living players** (ceil or standard “at least 50%” —
   use: votes ≥ living/2, rounding such that e.g. 6 living needs **3**), **and**
2. Votes are **strictly greater** than any other nomination’s vote total **today**.

### Ties

If two (or more) nominations tie for highest valid total, **nobody** is executed
from that contest (neither is “about to be executed” as sole leader).

### After a provisional leader

When someone has a valid leading tally, further nominations may still occur.
A later nomination with a **strictly higher** valid total becomes the new leader.

### Closing nominations

Storyteller: last call → countdown. No more nominations → execute the current leader
(if any). Only then does that player die (unless an ability prevents death on execution).

## Execution resolution

1. Kill the executed player (unless ability says they survive execution).
2. Flip public life token; place vote token on them (ghost vote reminder).
3. Shroud their Grimoire token; they lose ability (unless text says otherwise).
4. **Do not** reveal character or alignment.
5. Check win conditions.
6. If game continues → Night.

### Execution vs death

- **Execution** is the town’s formal act; **death** is the state change.
- Some abilities execute without killing, or kill without executing.
- An executed dead player still “uses up” the day’s single execution slot if the
  group chooses to execute a corpse (rare; still allowed by rules).

## Exile (Travellers only)

Travellers are not executed; they are **exiled** via a separate day process.
Exile does not count as the day’s execution. Travellers usually do not count
toward the two-alive evil win. See Traveller docs when implemented.
