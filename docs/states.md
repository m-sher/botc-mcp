# States

States are independent properties of a **player** (not only of a character token).
A player can be e.g. dead + poisoned, or drunk + good Townsfolk.

## Life: Alive | Dead

See [death-and-ghosts.md](death-and-ghosts.md).

## Alignment: Good | Evil

- Character tokens have a default alignment color, but **alignment is independent**.
- A Townsfolk can become evil; a Demon can become good (other scripts).
- When alignment flips, Storyteller informs the player in secret ASAP
  (not blocked by drunk/poison).
- Exception designs: Drunk / Lunatic do not know their true identity.

## Character

- Independent of alignment.
- Changing character grants the new ability immediately; old ability ends.
- Mid-game character change: setup bracket text `[like this]` does **not** re-apply.
- Becoming Minion/Demon mid-game does **not** reveal other evil players.

## Drunk | Sober and Poisoned | Healthy

**Drunk and poisoned do the same mechanical thing.**

### Effects

- Player **has no ability**.
- Storyteller still wakes them and pretends the ability works.
- Information abilities may receive **false** info (Storyteller’s choice; usually should).
- Once-per-game ability used while drunk/poisoned is **spent and failed**.
- Do **not** tell the player they are drunk/poisoned.
- Always give correct answers about **rules** questions.

### Independence

- Drunk + poisoned do not cancel; player is both.
- Ability used **on** a drunk/poisoned player still works (the actor’s ability resolves;
  the target’s inability is separate).
  - Empath correctly reads drunk neighbors’ alignment.
  - Fortune Teller correctly detects a poisoned Demon.
- Persistent effects pause while the **source** is drunk/poisoned and resume if
  they recover with time remaining (context-dependent; see almanac).
- Effects that **trigger while** drunk/poisoned are wasted (no retroactive fix).

### Who is drunk/poisoned?

The **player**, not the token identity. Character swaps keep the player’s drunk/poison status.

## Madness (mainly Sects & Violets)

- Social state: trying to convince the group that X is true (or avoiding that).
- Storyteller judges sincerity; may apply character-defined penalties/bonuses
  (e.g. Mutant execution risk, Cerenovus forced claim).
- Players are never forced to speak; they may accept the penalty instead.

## Registering as something else

Some characters **register** differently to detection abilities:

| Character | Registration |
| --- | --- |
| **Recluse** | May register as evil / Minion / Demon (Storyteller chooses per detection) |
| **Spy** | May register as good / Townsfolk / Outsider (Storyteller chooses per detection) |
| **Fortune Teller red herring** | One good player registers as Demon **to the Fortune Teller only** |

Registration is not a real alignment or character change.
